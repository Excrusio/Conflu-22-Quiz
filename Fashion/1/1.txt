For her wedding, Queen Victoria did something that some people suggest was to showcase her symbol of sexual purity, few historians suggest that it was more of a status symbol. Although not the first one to do so, Queen Victoria popularised it which is now common (almost mandatory) in every Christian wedding. Since photography techniques were not fully developed during the time of her wedding, a re-enactment of her wedding was done after more than a decade in which she showcased the thing once again.
