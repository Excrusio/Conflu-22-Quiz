X is a 1995 Thriller/Science Fiction novel by Indian author Y based loosely on real events in the life of Sir Ronald Ross - the Nobel Prize winning scientist who achieved a breakthrough in malaria research in 1898. The book's plot deals with an underground scientific movement where disciples of the movement can secure a form of immortality by transferring their chromosomes into another's body, and gradually becoming that person or taking over that person. The book was also the first by an Indian author to win the Arthur C. Clarke Award for best science fiction novel in 1997.
